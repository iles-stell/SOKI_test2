About : Why Confluence?

Created by Administrator, last modified by Andrew Constable on Aug 13, 2013
Confluence has been chosen as the platform at present because:
  • Portability - both the entire wiki instance and individual spaces/pages can be exported to XML (the contents could be exported to an alternative web site or wiki if needed)
  • Source code - available for licenced customers
  • Usage - Confluence is already widely used by the scientific community
  • User friendly - anyone who has used a word processor before will find creation and editing of pages very straightforward
  • Extendable - a wide range of third party plugins are available to extend the functionality of Confluence, and custom apps can be easily integrated into the platform
  • Support - Confluence comes with a commercial support agreement, and has an active support and development community
  • Features - Confluence supported most of the basic functionality required for SOKI's operations
  • Permissions - secure management of pages that need protection during development by a small number of the community

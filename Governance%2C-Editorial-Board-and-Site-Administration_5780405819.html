About : Governance, Editorial Board and Site Administration

Created by Shavawn Donoghue, last modified by Administrator on Apr 07, 2014

SOKI is currently established through the Antarctic Climate and Ecosystems Cooperative Research Centre, Hobart.  A Managing Editor is responsible for its administration and development as well as overseeing the Editorial Board.  The Managing Editor is responsible to the Chief Scientist of the Australian Antarctic Science Program.  The Managing Editor is supported by a Steering Committee that oversees the policies, development and direction of SOKI.

 

Roles and responsibilities

Steering Committee

  • Approve the development and maintenance of the strategic plan and, based on reports from the Managing Editor, to monitor progress against it.
  • Oversee the performance of the Editorial Board and Managing Editor 
  • Promote engagement in the wiki
  • Advise on governance of the wiki and its budget
  • Identify opportunities for partnerships in wiki development, promotion and funding
  • Participate in meetings by correspondence approx once every six months.  Papers and reports coordinated by the Managing Editor.

Managing Editor

  • Responsible for appointment and management of the Site Administrator and Content Manager
  • Responsible for site management
  • Manages the Editorial Board
  • Reports to the Steering Committee
  • Coordinates papers and reports for Steering Committee
  • Advises the Steering Committee on strategic direction and development of the wiki
  • Managing the budget
  • Obtaining funding
  • Reporting to funding bodies
  • Participate in meetings as required, including Steering Committee, Editorial Board.

Site Administrator

  • Maintain functionality of site – make sure all functions are working, troubleshooting problems.
  • Liaison with Confluence - licences, software, plugins.
  • Maintain software currency.
  • Maintain budget.
  • Maintain users, groups.
  • Technical development and implementation.
  • Moderates troubleshooting discussion forum.
  • With Senior Editors, implements recommendations from the Editorial Board

Content Manager

  • Develop and maintain protocols, processes and policies for content generation and management
  • Develop and implement appropriate content standards, e.g. template design 
  • With Site Administrator, administration of permissions and access to wiki
  • With Senior Editors, implements recommendations from the Editorial Board
  • Troubleshooting point of contact for Senior Content Editors and Content Editors
  • Oversee wiki content to ensure consistency of terminology and use
  • Advises Managing Editor on strategic content management

Editorial Board 

  • Responsible for overall wiki management
  • Strategic development of wiki
  • Dispute resolution
  • Aiming for longer term membership (compared with content editors, which may have high turnover).
  • Participation in Editorial Board meetings by correspondence approx once every six months.  Papers and reports coordinated by the Managing Editor in consultation with Chief Domain Editor.

Chief Domain Editor

  • Overseas the development of SOKI content, including the development of the domains and linkages between domains
  • Not involved in content management
  • Provide guidance to Domain Editors
  • In consultation with Domain Editors, consider the long-term strategies for managing content and domain development
  • Participation in Editorial Board meetings approximately every six months (activity updates and page review discussions)

 Domain Editors 

  • Point of contact for the domain
  • Oversees the structure and development of domain content
  • Not involved in content management
  • Provide guidance to Senior Content editors
  • In consultation with the Senior Content editors, invite submissions to the domain
  • In consultation with the Senior Content editors, invite consolidation of aspects of the domain
  • Participation in Editorial Board meetings approximately every six months and Domain meetings every three months

Senior Content Editors

  • Manages parts of the wiki and/or an area of expertise as agreed with Domain Editor
  • Receive notification of creation of new pages
  • Receives submissions of pages for peer-review
  • Assigns content review to Content Editors
  • Appoints content editors as required to manage subspaces
  • Ensures content is appropriate
  • Encourages appropriate revision of pages 
  • Suggests high quality pages that have achieved an high level of community engagement, consideration and acceptance for review?
  • Monitors content submitted to the Wiki for appropriateness, relevance and safety
  • Writing appropriate content for inclusion in the Wiki if required
  • Dispute resolution/referral to the Editorial Board
  • Approving wiki pages following peer-review
  • Consult with Content Manager on issues of standards, templates and structure 
  • Participate in meetings of Senior Content Editors in the Domain approximately every three months

Content Editors

Content editors are invited to participate by Senior Content Editors. 

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Manages content assigned by Senior Content Editors
  • Monitors content in area of expertise submitted to the Wiki for appropriateness, relevance and safety
  • Writing appropriate content for inclusion in the Wiki if required
  • Consult with Content Manager on issues of standards, templates and structure

Page Author

Wiki community participation guidelines; Polar Information Commons

  • That submitted materials are already in the public domain, or that they have clear rights to make these materials openly accessible through the wiki;
  • That these contributions are accessible and searchable online;
  • That, if requested, they will make reasonable efforts to provide additional information about their contributed materials, e.g., to help document the quality of their submitted materials and to ensure their long-term usability.
  • That they will make reasonable efforts to provide appropriate notification to the community of any significant errors in their contributed materials or descriptions, if any are discovered after submission.
